ceylon tea - health benefits


As mentioned before, Ceylon Tea is known around the world for it's amazing medicinal qualities. The right cup of tea can will not only refresh and revitalize you but may also prevent a whole plethora of diseases. the antioxidants Ceylon Tea is rich with may go as far as killing off cancer cells yet to pose a threat to your well-being and improve organ function all while making sure you feel rejuvenated and fresh.


Listed below are just a few of the many health benefits a drinker of Ceylon Tea is presented with.






Helps in Keeping your Heart Healthy

Ceylon teas contain higher levels of potassium when compared with tea grown in other regions. Potassium is extremely important for the health of your body, especially your heart as it relaxes your veins and arteries. This reduces your blood pressure and reduces the strain on your heart. In addition to this, some of the active ingredients in Ceylon Tea can reduce bad cholesterol. Furthermore, catechins, one of the ingredients in green Ceylon tea can help strengthen your arteries while also reducing the hardening of arteries to prevent damage and clogging.




Helps control Diabetes

Ceylon tea can help regulate diabetes and can help prevent diabetes. When compared to tea grown in other regions, Ceylon green tea help reduce the risk of developing type-2 diabetes. In addition, Ceylon tea can help regulate insulin and blood sugar levels, which reduces the risk of developing diabetes. Furthermore, it helps those with diabetes avoid dangerous spikes and drops in blood sugar.




Aids in weight loss

Ceylon teas, similar to other tea around the world can help you lose weight. Firstly, Ceylon green tea extract can help weight loss by reducing fat formation. And increasing fat breakdown. Furthermore, tea in general, including Ceylon tea can boost your metabolism. When your metabolism increases, your body burns fat faster and more efficiently. Improves cognitive function

studies have shown that drinking tea in general, including Ceylon tea, improves brain health. A recent study led by researchers from the National University of Singapore (NUS) revealed that regular tea drinkers have better organised brain regions -- and this is associated with healthy cognitive function -- compared to non-tea drinkers.
